abstract: "Geographically distributed scientific collaborations increasingly demand data access performance that approaches local storage speeds, regardless of where the data physically resides. In this paper we present the design and evaluation of the transparent geo-distribution layer in Onedata, which combines replica management, on-the-fly replication triggered by data-access patterns, and a transfer scheduling algorithm that minimises inter-site latency. We show how Spaces and Providers cooperate to provide automatic placement of replicas close to active compute resources. Measurements conducted on a pan-European testbed confirm that sequential throughput matches local disk performance for large files, while metadata operations scale linearly with the number of Oneproviders."
